Our use of cookies & local storage

At CineMax, we believe in transparency. This policy explains how we use cookies, local storage, and similar technologies to enhance your cinematic experience — exactly as implemented in our application (app.js). By using CineMax, you consent to the storage and access of data as described below.

1. What are cookies & local storage?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device. Local storage (used heavily by CineMax) is a modern web API that allows websites to store structured data persistently in your browser. Unlike cookies, local storage is not automatically sent to the server with every request — it stays on your device, and we use it to remember your preferences, wishlist, and viewing progress.

2. Storage keys & purposes

Our JavaScript (app.js) uses the following storage keys. All data remains on your device unless you clear it.

cinemax_wishlist Movies/TV shows you've saved ❤️ — used to display your wishlist.
cinemax_continue_watching Progress (timestamp/duration) for videos you started, so you can resume.
cinemax_watch_history Last 50 items you played — powers "continue watching" & AI recommendations.
cinemax_cache Cached movie metadata (posters, details) to reduce API calls and speed up browsing.
cinemax_new_content Tracks which movies/TV shows are marked "NEW" based on release date (7‑day window).
backend_connection (memory cache) Temporary in‑memory cache for server availability (refreshed often).

We do not use tracking cookies for advertising. All data is strictly for functional and personalization purposes.

3. Third-party services

While CineMax itself uses only local storage, third-party services we integrate may set their own cookies or storage:

  • YouTube (trailers): Embeds may set cookies for video preferences and analytics. Google's privacy policy applies.
  • The Movie Database (TMDB): API requests are made from your browser; TMDB may log requests according to their policy.
  • Streaming servers (Vidlux): Embedded iframes may use local storage or cookies for playback functionality. These are outside our control.

4. Managing your storage

You have full control over your data. Here's how to clear it:

  • Clear wishlist/progress: Use your browser's "Clear site data" option (usually found in DevTools → Application → Local Storage).
  • Opt out of local storage: You can disable local storage in browser settings, but CineMax may not function properly (wishlist, continue watching, etc. will not persist).
  • Reset "new" badges: Clear cinemax_new_content from local storage.

For a quick reset, you can also run localStorage.clear() in the browser console (this will log you out of any local preferences).

5. Performance & caching

To deliver a smooth experience, CineMax caches API responses (like movie lists) in memory and local storage. This data expires automatically (usually after 1 hour) and is never shared. Server latency checks are temporary and not stored beyond your session.

6. Changes to this policy

We may update this Cookie Policy to reflect changes in our app or legal requirements. The "Last Updated" date at the top of this page will be revised. Continued use of CineMax after changes constitutes your acceptance.
